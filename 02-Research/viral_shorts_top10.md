# Top 10 Actionable Viral Shorts Findings
**Distilled from full research — March 2026**
**Full analysis:** `viral_shorts_analysis.md`

---

## #1 — The Hook Must Land Before Second 2, Not Second 3

Everyone says "3 seconds." The real threshold is 2 seconds. Shorts with an immediate hook in the first 2 seconds retain 19% more viewers. The algorithm seeds your video to ~100–500 people; if they swipe, the video never reaches the main feed. Target a Viewed vs. Swiped Away (VVSA) rate of 70%+ or distribution collapses.

**Action:** First spoken word within 0.5 seconds of video start. Opening visual = most striking frame in the video. Never lead with music, logos, or "hey guys."

---

## #2 — Looping Structure = Free Views and Algorithm Fuel

As of March 31, 2025, YouTube counts every replay as an additional view. A looping Short can hit 300%+ Average Percentage Viewed (APV) — this signals massive quality to the algorithm. Average Shorts retention is 73%; looping Shorts regularly exceed 100%.

**Action:** Write the ending first. Make the final line echo/recontextualize the opening line. Final visual frame should mirror opening frame. Eliminate CTAs (they break the loop). Keep under 25 seconds for maximum loop potential.

---

## #3 — Caption Specs That Maximize Retention

Captions improve completion rate by 12–15%. ~60% of Shorts are watched without sound; ~80% of Gen Z uses subtitles always.

**Exact specs:**
- Font: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Arial, Roboto)
- Size: 20–22pt (never below 18pt)
- Position: Center third of screen — avoid top 20% and bottom 25%
- Words per line: 4–6 words (max 7, max 42 characters)
- Duration: (word count × 0.3 sec) + 0.5 sec = display time
- Example: 5-word line = 2.0 seconds on screen
- Style: White text on black semi-transparent box (60–80% opacity), 4.5:1 contrast minimum
- Pre-roll: Caption appears 0.1–0.3 seconds before the audio

---

## #4 — Optimal Length Is Bimodal, Not a Straight Curve

Research on 35 billion views found YouTube Shorts perform best at either ~13 seconds or ~60 seconds. The 30–45 second middle range underperforms. For TikTok, the old 21–34 second sweet spot is shifting — the platform now rewards 60–180 seconds for substantive content.

**Action for history content:** Aim for 45–60 seconds on YouTube Shorts (story arc + loop point); 30–45 seconds on TikTok (tighter, faster). A Short with 85% completion at 30 seconds outranks one with 70% completion at 60 seconds.

---

## #5 — Shares > Saves > Comments > Likes (Algorithm Weight Order)

Platforms have made this explicit. A like takes 0.5 seconds and means nothing. A share means someone attached their personal reputation to your content. TikTok now treats saves and shares as the primary quality signals. Instagram confirmed "sends per reach" (DM shares) outweighs all other signals.

**Action:** Create content explicitly designed to be shared — content that makes the viewer say "I need to send this to someone." History facts work perfectly: "Wait until your friend hears about this." End with a shareable moment, not a call to subscribe.

---

## #6 — History Content Formula That Drives Shares and Rewatches

Proven framework:
1. Hook about a specific person + specific time pressure ("48 hours," "one decision," "the night before")
2. Tension escalation through the mid-section
3. Surprising reveal or twist
4. "Why this still matters today" bridge to modern relevance OR loop back to hook

Topics with highest proven virality: Ancient Rome (1.8B+ trend views), "Day in the Life" ordinary people content (3x more comments/shares vs. battles/rulers), "What you weren't taught about X," events that feel eerily modern.

**Never lead with dates or names alone.** Lead with stakes.

---

## #7 — Cut Speed Formula: Aggressive Open, Earned Patience Later

- First 5 seconds: cut every 0.8–1.5 seconds (maximum urgency)
- Content body: cut every 2–3 seconds once viewer is committed
- Something must change visually every gridline — b-roll cut, text change, or camera cut
- B-roll clips: 1.5–2.5 seconds each in the content section
- Never let a single shot sit static while narration continues

For history content: opening b-roll must be cinematic and dramatic (not a map that slowly zooms). Save the slow, moody wide shots for the emotional payoff section.

---

## #8 — Voiceover Pacing for History: 150 WPM with Strategic Pauses

Educational content: 140–160 WPM baseline. Dramatic history delivery: 120–140 WPM at key moments, with intentional pauses of 0.5–1.5 seconds before key reveals. The pause before the reveal is as powerful as the reveal itself.

Tone: Confident, urgent, documentary narrator — NOT academic professor. Think "National Geographic narrator who's also a little horrified." Speed up slightly (1.1–1.2x) in background-delivery sections; slow down + lower pitch at emotional peaks.

Music: Epic orchestral or cinematic suspense bed at 20–30% volume under narration. Never upbeat pop for history content — tonal mismatch destroys completion.

---

## #9 — Kinetic Typography Rules for History Shorts

Animate 5–8 key moments per 20–30 second video. Animate at the phrase level, not word-by-word (word-by-word bouncing feels frantic). Best animations for history: slide-in for new information, pop/scale for numbers and key names, type-on effect for suspense reveals.

**What to never do:** Mix 3+ animation styles in one video. Animate filler words. Use animations that take longer than 150ms to resolve. Have text stay on screen after the viewer has read it.

Add a subtle SFX (impact hit, pop, or bass hit) to coincide with animated text reveals — the audio-visual combo increases recall and rewatch.

---

## #10 — Algorithm Differs Significantly Between Platforms — Treat Them Separately

| Signal | YouTube Shorts | TikTok |
|---|---|---|
| Most important | Completion rate / APV | Watch time + shares |
| Distribution | Slow burn (weeks–months) | Fast spike (24–48 hrs) |
| Trending audio | Minor boost | Major boost — use it |
| Content evergreen? | Yes — search-surfaced | No — recency matters |
| Loop rewarded? | Yes — replays = views | Yes — replays weighted |

**Action:** Same raw content, different edits. YouTube Shorts: lean into looping structure, evergreen topics, completion rate. Optimize for 45–60 sec. TikTok: trim to 30–45 sec, use trending audio as bed, lean into trending topics/sounds for distribution spike.

Post both simultaneously — repurpose with a slight timing/audio adjustment, don't just cross-post the same file.
