# Risk Management

> [[00-INDEX|← Back to Index]]

---

## The Golden Rule

> **Only trade with money you can afford to lose entirely — most memecoins go to zero**

---

## Portfolio Allocation

```
Max total portfolio in memecoins: 5%
Max single memecoin trade:        1–2% of total portfolio

Example ($1,000 portfolio):
  Total in memecoins: $50 max
  Single trade:       $10–20 max
```

---

## Position Sizing by Phase

| Phase | Per Trade |
|-------|----------|
| Learning (new to this) | 0.05–0.10 SOL |
| Comfortable | 0.10–0.50 SOL |
| Advanced | 0.50–2 SOL |

> Diversify: **5–10 small positions > 1 large position**

---

## Take-Profit Ladder (Use Axiom TPSL)

```
TP1: Sell 25% of position at 2x  → recover entry, now playing with house money
TP2: Sell 25% of position at 5x  → lock in strong profit
TP3: Sell 25% of position at 10x → major win
Ride: Let last 25% go to zero or moon
```

> **Never let a 5x ride back to a loss without taking some off**

---

## The 2x Rule

When a coin doubles:
1. Sell enough to recover your initial investment
2. You're now playing with house money
3. Psychological freedom — hold or sell the rest without stress

---

## Stop-Loss Strategy

```
Standard:  -30% from entry
Strict:    -50% from entry
Immediate: Dev dumps, LP pulled, Telegram goes silent → EXIT regardless of price
```

- **Set your stop BEFORE you buy**, not after
- Use Axiom's built-in TPSL to automate stops
- Don't move your stop loss lower to avoid taking a loss (discipline)

---

## When to Exit Entirely (Override TP/SL)

- Dev wallet makes large sell (check Solscan)
- Liquidity drops > 30% suddenly
- Telegram goes silent or dev disappears
- Volume collapses with no news catalyst
- A KOL who called it starts selling
- Token has been running 4–6+ hours without new buyers
- You've hit your 5x–10x target

---

## Mental Framework

- Treat every trade as potentially going to zero
- Wins are bonuses, not expectations
- Keep a trade journal: entry, exit, reason, outcome
- Review weekly: what worked, what didn't
- Never revenge trade after a loss
