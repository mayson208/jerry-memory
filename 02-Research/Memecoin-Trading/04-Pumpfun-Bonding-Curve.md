# Pump.fun & Bonding Curve Mechanics

> [[00-INDEX|← Back to Index]]

---

## How Pump.fun Works

- Anyone launches a token for ~0.02 SOL
- Token starts on a **bonding curve** — price increases as supply is bought
- 800M of 1B total supply on the bonding curve
- Remaining 200M goes to LP at graduation
- No team allocation, no presale — fully fair launch

---

## Bonding Curve Explained

Think of it like a vending machine that gets more expensive as inventory shrinks.

- Price increases exponentially as more tokens are bought
- Starting price: fractions of a cent
- **Graduation threshold: ~$69,000 market cap** (800M tokens sold)
- At graduation → liquidity auto-deployed to DEX

---

## Graduation (2025 Update)

- **Before March 2025:** Graduated tokens went to Raydium
- **After March 2025:** Pump.fun launched **PumpSwap** (their own DEX)
- PumpSwap fees: 0.25% per trade (0.2% to LPs, 0.05% to protocol)
- Post-graduation price set by AMM (buyers/sellers), not bonding curve

---

## What Graduation Means for Price

- Token becomes tradeable on open AMM with real liquidity
- Visible on DEX aggregators (Dexscreener, Birdeye) = new buyers discover it
- Historical pattern: **2x–5x spike on graduation**, then volatile
- Brings in conservative traders who only buy graduated tokens

---

## The Numbers That Matter

- **~98.7% of Pump.fun tokens never graduate** — die on the curve
- Only ~1.3% graduate — these are the signal
- ~93% of graduated tokens show pump-and-dump patterns
- The ~7% that run can go 10x–1000x

## Best Entry Points

1. **Catch at 1–5 min** if bundle/sniper metrics are clean and narrative is strong
2. **Final Stretch** — buy when bonding curve is at 80–99%, sell into graduation pump
3. **Just graduated** — first 10–30 minutes post-graduation, before price fully discovers itself
4. **Post-graduation dip** — if coin dips after graduation pump and holds above 50% of pump high
