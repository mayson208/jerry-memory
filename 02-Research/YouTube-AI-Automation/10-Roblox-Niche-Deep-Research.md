## 1. CPM & Revenue

### Raw Numbers
- **Gaming CPM (advertiser pays):** $4–$15
- **Gaming RPM (creator receives after YouTube 45% cut):** $2–$7
- **Roblox specifically:** Sits at the **low end** of gaming — estimated $2–$4 RPM for general audience content
- **"Made for Kids" labeled content:** CPM collapses to **$0.25–$0.35** — effectively demonetized (~95% revenue drop vs. unlabeled content)

### vs. Other Gaming Niches

### Key Insight
Roblox channels that avoid the "Made for Kids" label and produce content clearly aimed at teens/general audiences (challenges, commentary, meta content, news) earn **2–10x more** than channels labeled for kids. Revenue strategy must prioritize **mixed or older audience framing**.

### Key Rule
**Retention over length.** A 12-minute video at 60% retention outperforms a 30-minute video at 30% retention. The 8–15 minute range is the sweet spot for most Roblox formats in terms of monetization + algorithm performance.

## 6. Thumbnail Style

### Proven High-CTR Formula for Roblox
- **Colors:** Neon/vibrant — electric blue, glowing green, hot pink, bright yellow-orange. High contrast against dark or blurred backgrounds.
- **Faces:** Always include a face (avatar or creator's face). Exaggerated expressions — shock, fear, excitement, huge eyes. Faces outperform object-only thumbnails by **25–30%**.
- **Composition:** Single focal subject centered or slightly left, blurred background for depth. Avoid clutter.
- **Text overlay:** 1–4 words max, bold/chunky font, capitalized, contrasting outline. One clear promise ("NO JUMP," "STARTER ONLY," "$1,000,000 SPENT").
- **Stakes signal:** The thumbnail should communicate a constraint, a risk, or a reward — viewers need to feel something is at stake.
- **Branding:** Consistent color palette and character across channel for recognition in feeds.

### Formula Summary
> **[Shocked avatar face] + [Neon background] + [3-word bold text = constraint/reward/shock]**

## 7. Script / Voice Style

### What Works
- **Tone:** Energetic, upbeat, slightly dramatic — matches the game's fast-paced visual style
- **Pacing:** Fast delivery in action moments, slower for reveals/punchlines
- **Kid-friendly but not babyish:** Top monetized channels pitch to teens, not young children — avoids "Made for Kids" flag
- **Hook in first 5 seconds:** State the challenge/premise immediately. No long intros.
- **Callbacks and payoffs:** Scripts with a setup-tension-resolution arc outperform raw gameplay narration
- **Humor:** Self-deprecating, reaction-based humor (not edgy)
- **Call to action:** Embedded naturally mid-video, not just end-screen

### Voice Delivery for AI
- AI TTS tools that work for Roblox: Speechactors, Murf, ElevenLabs (for cloned/custom voice), Speechify
- Target voice profile: **Energetic male or female teen voice, 1.1–1.2x natural pace, warm tone**
- Add subtle sound effects under narration for immersion
- Avoid robotic monotone — use pitch variation and emphasis

## 9. Monetization Challenges

### The "Made for Kids" Problem
This is the **#1 monetization risk for Roblox channels.**

| Status | CPM | Features Lost |
|--------|-----|--------------|
| Not Made for Kids | $4–$15 | Full monetization |
| Made for Kids | $0.25–$0.35 | Comments, personalized ads, memberships, merch shelf, end screens, notification bell |

- Revenue drop when labeled MFK: **~95%**
- YouTube (and FTC) can audit and force the label if content is clearly child-directed
- Disney was fined $10M in 2025 for mislabeling

### Risk Factors That Trigger "Made for Kids" Classification
- Animated/cartoon characters prominently featured
- Child voices or child presenters
- Toy or game content with no adult framing
- Simple language, bright colors, no complex themes

### How Top Channels Avoid It
- Creator uses their own adult/teen voice as primary narrator
- Content framed as "challenge" or "commentary" rather than kids' show
- Titles use terms like "I tried..." or "I survived..." — adult first-person framing
- Channel description mentions "gaming channel for teens and adults"
- Avoid "for kids" in titles/descriptions

### COPPA 2.0 (Not Yet Law as of 2026)
- Would extend protections to ages 13–16
- If passed, would further restrict targeted ads for minors
- Roblox itself is lobbying in favor of COPPA 2.0 — controversial among creators (KreekCraft publicly opposed this)
- Not yet enacted but watch this space

### Other Demonetization Triggers
- Profanity (yellow dollar icon, limited ads)
- Controversial topics
- Copyright music in gameplay footage
- "Repetitious content" — AI-generated videos must provide unique value per YouTube's July 2025 policy update

## 11. Competition Level

### Honest Assessment: HIGH Competition, But Niching Works

- Roblox is **one of the most competitive gaming niches on YouTube**
- Top channels have billions of views and 10M+ subscribers (KreekCraft, LankyBox, Denis)
- General "Roblox gameplay" is extremely saturated

### Where There's Still Room
- **Specific game niches:** New/trending Roblox games before saturation (e.g., Dress to Impress hit 4B+ views before channels saturated it)
- **News/update channels:** Informational format, searchable, underserved by big entertainment channels
- **Teen/adult framing:** Most competition is kid-oriented; channels with older framing face less direct competition and earn more
- **Shorts-led strategy:** Roblox Shorts are still growing; 29.3M+ TikTok posts shows demand for short-form
- **Sub-game specialists:** A channel dedicated entirely to one game (e.g., only Brookhaven RP, only Adopt Me) builds algorithmic authority faster

### Verdict
New channels **can** grow in Roblox with a smart angle. Broad "Roblox gameplay" channels face near-impossible competition. Niche-within-niche strategy is required.
